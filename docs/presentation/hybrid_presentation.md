# Hybrid Presentation Draft

## Suggested flow

This version combines a strong narrative structure with enough product detail to feel credible and concrete.

---

## Slide 1. Title

**Title:**
Explainable Mapping for Integration Delivery

**Subtitle:**
From fragmented mapping work to a governed delivery outcome

**Key message:**
This approach helps teams move from raw structures to reviewed, explainable, and reusable mapping decisions.

---

## Slide 2. The problem

**Title:**
Why this matters

**Slide bullets:**
- schema mapping is still slow, fragmented, and hard to reuse
- teams often start from specs, SQL snapshots, and partial metadata
- one-off AI suggestions are hard to trust in delivery work
- mapping knowledge is often lost when the project moves on

**Key message:**
Integration delivery needs a more structured and governable way to move from raw input to reviewed decisions.

---

## Slide 3. What the solution provides

**Title:**
What the solution provides

**Slide bullets:**
- explainable mapping suggestions with review checkpoints
- governed artifacts and reusable semantic knowledge
- transformation starter code and preview output
- a BA Mapping Report for analyst handoff and delivery summary

**Key message:**
The solution does not stop at mapping suggestions; it helps teams produce a reviewable and reusable delivery outcome.

---

## Slide 4. Why it adds value

**Title:**
Why it adds value

**Slide bullets:**
- less rework across mapping and delivery cycles
- clearer review and approval decisions
- better traceability from source structure to target outcome
- stronger handoff from analysis to implementation

**Key message:**
The real value is not only better suggestions; it is less friction, more trust, and a stronger delivery package.

---

## Slide 5. Product surface

**Title:**
Main product areas

**Slide bullets:**
- Workspace
- Governance

**Key message:**
The product is already structured as a practical workbench, not as a single-purpose demo screen.

## Slide 6. BA Mapping Report

**Title:**
BA Mapping Report

**Slide bullets:**
- structured summary of the workspace outcome
- target object, grain, concept groups, and key decisions
- open risks, exclusions, and readiness signals
- a better basis for analyst handoff and later export as a document

**Key message:**
The BA Mapping Report turns the solution into a more business-facing artifact, not just a mapping engine.

---

## Slide 7. Trust and governance

**Title:**
Why the approach is credible

**Slide bullets:**
- deterministic-first mapping with bounded AI assistance
- review checkpoints before durable output
- governed mapping sets, reusable catalog assets, and benchmark history
- explicit control points instead of blind automation

**Key message:**
Trust comes from control, explainability, and reviewability.

---

## Slide 8. Typical workflow

**Title:**
How a team uses it

**Slide bullets:**
1. ingest source and target structures
2. generate and review mapping suggestions
3. inspect trust, canonical paths, and target contract
4. generate output, save governed artifacts, and reuse them later

**Key message:**
The workflow supports an end-to-end analyst loop, not only one ranking step.

---

## Slide 9. Closing

**Title:**
Executive summary

**Slide bullets:**
- the solution reduces rework in mapping-heavy delivery work
- it makes decisions explainable and reviewable
- it creates reusable output for analysts, reviewers, and implementation teams

**Closing line:**
This turns semantic integration work into a governed, reusable delivery capability.
